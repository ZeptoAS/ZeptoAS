Overview

The Zepto outboard engine aims to be a no-compromise, quiet and clean replacement to existing outboard engines.

The simplicity of an electric engine reduces the requirements of regular mainenance and oil changes.

Performance: The performance of an electric engine is superior to a gas engine in many ways. An electric engine gives a maximum torque across the whole RPM range while a gas powered engine only reaches maximum torque near wide-open throttle. This allows a boat powered with an electric engine to get into plane more quickly.

Input power = A drive’s power consumption. Often used as a performance indicator for electric outboards (current x voltage), expressed in watts or HP. Input power is not usually specified for combustion motors such as petrol outboards or diesel inboards. However, it can also be determined (flow rate of petrol or diesel x calorific value of petrol or diesel) and is also expressed in watts or HP.

Shaft Power = Power rating of petrol outboards, comparable with cars (torque x angular velocity). The rating is expressed in HP or kW but does not take propeller loss into account, which can vary between 30% and 80%.

Propulsive Power = Performance indicator used by commercial shipping and Torqeedo (thrust x speed). The rating is expressed in HP or kW and takes all losses into account, including propeller loss, and clearly indicates the actual power delivered by the drive system for propulsion.
